Design systems serve as foundational sets of rules and components—including fonts, UI elements, and layout guidelines—that ensure consistency and uniformity in software and website development. Analogous to a Lego set, these systems provide both the building blocks and the instructions necessary to create a familiar and user-friendly experience, regardless of who is performing the development work.

Context engineering refers to the process of preparing and providing AI agents with the specific information they need to succeed before they begin a task. The Model Context Protocol (MCP) acts as an industry-standard mechanism for sharing this information in a format that is easily consumed by AI models.

Agentic AI utilizes this context to make autonomous decisions, choose actions, and employ tools to accomplish real-world tasks. By connecting an AI agent to a design system via MCP, users can describe their requirements, and the agent will adhere strictly to the established design rules and components. While a standard AI might rely on memorized instructions, the use of MCP provides the agent with an authoritative source of truth, ensuring that software prototypes are built accurately and consistently.
